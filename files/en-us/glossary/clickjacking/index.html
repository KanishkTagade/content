Clickjacking is an interface-based attack that tricks website users into unwarily clicking on malicious links. In clickjacking, the attackers embed their malicious links into buttons or legitimate pages in a website. In an infected {{glossary("Site")}}, whenever a user clicks on a legitimate link, the attacker gets the confidential information of that user - which ultimately compromises the user's privacy on the Internet. Clickjacking falls under the A6 – Security Misconfiguration item in OWASP’s 2017 Top 10 list.

Clickjacking can be prevented by implementing a Content Security Policy (CSP) and other defense methods.
